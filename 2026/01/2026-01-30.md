# Even the Builders Hate It

Read [this insightful essay](https://blog.mikeswanson.com/backseat-software) by Mike Swanson. He explains in detail how we ended up with software that constantly interrupts us.

> "People use “enshittification” to describe platform decay. What I’m describing here is one of the mechanisms that makes that decay feel personal. It’s the constant conversion of your attention into a KPI."

Countless features started with good intent (read: *customer support*), but marketing misuse has twisted them into annoyance engines.

It’s not all doom and gloom, though. The essay ends with 5 helpful steps to fight back.

More people should follow them.
